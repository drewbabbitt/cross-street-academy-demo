Earning a diploma

Students who meet Cross Street Academy’s graduation requirements and fulfill their state’s graduation requirements receive a diploma. Because requirements vary from state to state, it is the parent’s responsibility to research their state’s requirements and ensure they are met.

Each college and university is also unique in what it looks for, so we recommend contacting the schools your student is interested in to determine what additional admission requirements, if any, exist.

The Bible requirement

To satisfy the Bible requirement, students must take at least .5 credit in three separate school years.

Earning credit and passing classes

A student must receive a D- or above to pass a class. A student who earns a D+ through a D- will receive high school credit and may be placed in the next level. Families should note, however, that many colleges do not recognize any grade below a C- for admissions purposes in any class. In some cases — math and Spanish classes, specifically — a student with a grade below a C- will need to remediate the course at parent expense to demonstrate proficiency in the skills required for success at the next level.
