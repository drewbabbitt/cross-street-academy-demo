How to apply

The application process

Five straightforward steps carry your family from a first conversation to confirmed enrollment.

1

Request more information

Want more than what’s on the website? Request more information and our Head of School will contact you personally by email or phone. You are also welcome to call us at (475) 477-0101.

2

Create a Cross Street Academy account

Ready to apply? Create an account through Gradelink — it only takes a minute.

3

Complete an application

Submit the online application through Gradelink: student and family information, your preferred payment option, required documentation, and a one-time, non-refundable $25 application fee.

4

Preview live classes (optional)

After applying, you may request to experience a class at CSA by viewing a class recording from one of our courses.

5

Acceptance to CSA

When the Head of School and family agree CSA is a good fit, the student is accepted and enrollment is finalized. Final admissions decisions are made after the entire application process is complete.

Before you apply

Additional enrollment information

As a 100% online school, CSA is unable to provide quality educational experiences for children with certain learning disabilities. Careful consideration is given to each applicant to ensure CSA’s program design is a good match for the applicant’s needs.

Each applicant completes an English and Math placement test to ensure correct course-level placement. Students may advance to an accelerated course if they meet the following requirements:

  • Entering 6th graders — Placement English and Math scores of 60 or higher on the 5th grade Placement Test
  • 8th graders — Placement Math score of 60 or higher on the Math 800 Placement Test
Non-Discrimination Policy. Cross Street Academy admits students of any race, color, national and ethnic origin to all the rights, privileges, programs, and activities generally accorded or made available to students at the school. It does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national or ethnic origin in administration of its educational policies, admissions policies, scholarship program, athletic and other school administered programs.
