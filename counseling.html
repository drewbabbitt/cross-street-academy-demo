Guidance Counseling Center

Guidance counseling at Cross Street Academy

The counseling center supports students in their educational, vocational, and social/emotional development — and serves high school students as a dedicated resource for class planning, college preparation, and career planning.

Guidance Counselor

Mrs. Emma Mitchell

Mrs. Mitchell received her master’s degree in clinical mental health counseling and her bachelor’s degree in psychology from Truett McConnell University. She has been working as a counselor in Christian private practice with children, teens, adults, and couples.

Her goal is to help each student navigate the struggles, pressures, and changes in their life through a biblical perspective.

Interested in scheduling a meeting?

Email the counseling center with your request and we will find a mutually convenient time to connect: emitchell@crossstreetacademy.com

Areas of Support

Guidance for every part of student life

Counseling at Cross Street reaches across three areas of a student’s development.

Educational development

Guidance that supports each student’s academic growth — including class planning for high school students as they chart their path toward graduation.

College & career planning

A resource for high school students in their vocational development: preparing for college and thinking carefully about the careers ahead of them.

Social & emotional development

Help navigating the struggles, pressures, and changes of student life — approached, always, through a biblical perspective.
